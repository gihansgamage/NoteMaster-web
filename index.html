Everything you need

All your study materials,
beautifully organised

Note Master brings PDFs, audio, web links, YouTube, images, and text into one smart, searchable space.

PDF Viewer — Built In
Open, scroll, zoom, and bookmark PDF pages right inside the app. No switching apps — your study documents live alongside your notes with pinch-to-zoom and page jump.
Smart Auto Summary
As you write, Note Master generates a clean summary from your body text, tags, and attachment types automatically.
Table of Contents
Markdown headings are parsed into an automatic, tappable table of contents for every note so you navigate long content effortlessly.
YouTube & Web Links
Paste a YouTube link and watch it embedded in the app. Add any web URL and browse it with a full in-app browser, no external app needed.
Audio Attachments
Attach lecture recordings or voice memos to any note and play them back with a simple in-note audio player.
Hashtag Categorisation
Add tags like #biology, #exam, or #revision. The editor even suggests tags used in the same notebook so your notes are always findable.
Subject Notebooks
Create subject-level notebooks (Biology, History, Design…) with accent colours. Each notebook has its own TOC and materials list tabs.
Share, Pin & Personalise
Share notes as formatted text, pin your most important notes to the home feed, personalise the app with your name and choose between light and dark mode from Settings.
Simple workflow

Four steps to organised brilliance

Everything is designed to get out of your way and help you focus on what matters — learning.

1
Create a notebook
Tap "Create Notebook" on the home screen. Give it a name like Biology or Work — it gets a unique accent colour automatically.
2
Add a note
Write a title, use Markdown for headings, add hashtags, and attach PDFs, images, audio, video, or links in one place.
3
Browse materials
Switch to the Materials tab in your notebook to see everything — open PDFs, watch YouTube videos, play audio — all without leaving the app.
4
Review via TOC & Summary
Your table of contents builds itself from headings. The auto-summary captures the key idea so you can review at a glance before an exam.
